# Pitch — flow & talking points

> Distilled from [`transcript.md`](./transcript.md). Target **2–3 min**. Tone: casual, funny,
> a little satirical. Hard-cap the length and rehearse.

**Who delivers what**
- **Yuva** — the narrative / storyline (intro hook → scale payoff → business close), **the judge
  interaction (Aaron/Sarah)**, and **the sponsor-video easter egg (§2)**. Main voice.
- **Devan** — the product feature walkthrough (mainly the product).
- **Holly** — wears the EEG headset the whole time (visual hook); demos the screens; **delivers the
  Fiber API / GTM closing beat (§6)**.

---

## 1. Hook / intro — **Yuva**
- Set the scene: *"We're bored B2B SaaS founders at the YC hackathon — so naturally we opened
  TikTok / Instagram Reels and started doom-scrolling."*
- The realization: *"We all noticed we like watching **Tech** content. No surprise — short
  attention spans. And we're at a **growth** hackathon… so why not marry the two?"*
- The turn: *"What if we used our own **brain data** to see WHY we like certain videos?"*
- **Visual hook:** someone (Holly) is already wearing the EEG — *"what is that thing on her head?"*
- Alt framing (optional): *"We're B2B founders with no clue about growth beyond numbers, so we
  built a device to actually see it."*

## 2. What we built — **Devan** (the product)
- *"We wore the device for the whole hackathon and watched a lot of videos — honestly the easy
  part."*
- **Devan walks the product:** the 3 screens — live scroll + live brainwave, the per-video
  analysis, the session report. Show the waveform **spiking on a hook, flat on a boring beat**.
  Devan carries the product explanation.

## 2b. Sponsor-video flex (easter egg) — **Yuva**
- Yuva jumps in: *"…and we even ran your **own** launch videos through it — here's why Codex's /
  Cursor's hook lands, and where it loses people."* Judges recognize their own content → the room
  leans in.
- Keep it to **1–2 sponsor clips**; the real demo dataset is actual Tech UGC, not these (they're
  landscape product videos, off-format — see PRD content note).

## 3. Judge interaction — **Yuva**  *(the differentiator — few teams will do this)*
- *"But we got curious — what do the **judges** like?"* → ask **Aaron** to put on the headset,
  watch a couple clips.
- Show **altered iterations** of similar clips (different visual elements, talking speed, text,
  subtitles) → *"here's exactly which hooks Aaron's brain responded to."*
- **Be specific** (use the 5-field schema): not just "Aaron likes Tech UGC" — name the talking
  speed / style / format he spiked on. Specificity = trust.
- Then **Sarah** → compare. *"So who's got better video taste — Aaron or Sarah?"* (funny
  competition; do the head-to-head only if there's time).

## 4. Scale / payoff — **Yuva**
- *"This is only a limited data set — 3 of us, ~10 videos, 5 minutes."*
- *"With more data — ours + the population's — we can predict and explain why **anyone** likes a
  video, in real time."*
- *"That's a hyper-specialized, direct pathway to virality — instead of throwing a dart at a
  blind wall."*

## 5. Business close — **Yuva** (Devan backs the metrics)
- The status quo: *"UGC marketers spray and pray — hire 100 creators at ~$100/video, dump a month
  of content into production, then wait to see what hits the algorithm."*
- Our value: *"We **reduce the number of darts you throw** to hit virality — using your own
  consumer psychology to hit the nail on the head."*
- The metric line (judges all know these): *"Less wasted content → **CPM ↓ → CAC ↓** → we save
  your bottom line."*
- The kicker: ***"Stop spraying and praying. Use science to go viral"*** — *"so your friends
  don't clown you (get less chopped) for not going viral."*

## 6. Closing easter egg — Fiber AI / GTM (optional, ~15s) — **Holly**
- Holly closes: *"And who do we sell this to? We used **Fiber** (a sponsor) — literally one
  `peopleSearch` call — to pull the Heads of Growth at UGC brands. That's our outbound list. We know
  what goes viral; Fiber tells us who to sell it to."*
- Holly can flash the **actual API call** (`peopleSearch`, `x-api-key`) on screen for ~2s as proof
  we really used it — ties the sponsor in concretely.
- One slide, one call. Sponsor eligibility without muddling the story. **First thing to cut if long.**
- ⚠️ Do **not** pitch Fiber as "find/recruit the creator" — the people in our clips are founders,
  and editors aren't in a B2B database. GTM is the only honest fit.
